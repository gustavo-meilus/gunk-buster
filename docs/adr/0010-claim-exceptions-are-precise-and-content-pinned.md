# Claim exceptions are precise and content-pinned

A legitimate Radar claim is excepted by a Chief decision scoped to the document, check, exact normalized token, required reason, and current document content hash. The exception remains in persisted and JSON output with disposition `EXCEPTED` and its reason, but leaves active finding counts and patch plans; human output may summarize it separately. It expires and becomes active again when that document changes. Decisions live in a tool-owned, Git-tracked `.gunk-buster/claim-exceptions.json` ledger and are authored through an explicit CLI action against a persisted Radar finding; the tool records the scope, reason, hash, and decision time, so Chiefs never calculate hashes or edit product configuration by hand. We chose this over inline suppression comments, whole-document exclusion, or broad token allowlists so exceptional intent can be recorded without creating durable blind spots or suppression residue inside agent-readable content.
